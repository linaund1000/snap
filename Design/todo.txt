================================================================================
 NOTES FROM DESIGN DISCUSSIONS
================================================================================

- Facial expressions: DON'T over-detail! It's too much work for isometric view.
  Focus on EMOJI-SPREADING style, body movements, and exaggerated gestures.
  
- Good rigging + kimono/clothing usage is more important than face detail.

- Map layout: League of Legends style camera. Trees can partially block view,
  but ALL buildings/architecture must be short. Characters must ALWAYS be visible.
  Markets, cafes, public places slightly set back from main view.

- Social map is critical: who is friend, who is enemy, connection strength.

- Main character is a newcomer — must introduce self, earn trust, work for living.
  If all NPCs hate the player, they flee from camera = can't take pictures = can't work.

- Day/month system: sun rises, goes down, night starts. Time keeps ticking.

- FSM is the most critical technical component. Must be strict, well-tested,
  written in separate folder, independently runnable.

- Basics must work first: characters don't fall through ground, no collisions
  breaking, walk/run/look must be natural. Use outsource solutions (Mixamo etc.)
  wherever possible to save time.

- Hello world first! Move, go, do work — verify scripts before connecting systems.
